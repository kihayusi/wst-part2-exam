Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

⏱️ Prep Time: 10 mins | Cook Time: 15 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 200g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 100g Pecorino Romano cheese, finely grated
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, finely grated
  • Black pepper, freshly ground
  • Salt for pasta water

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Add spaghetti and cook according to package directions until al dente.
  2. While pasta cooks, place diced pancetta in a cold skillet. Turn heat to medium and cook until crispy and golden, about 8-10 minutes.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, Pecorino Romano, Parmesan, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining. Drain the spaghetti.
  5. Remove skillet from heat. Add drained pasta to the pancetta and toss to combine.
  6. Pour the egg mixture over the hot pasta and toss quickly, adding reserved pasta water gradually to create a creamy sauce.
  7. Serve immediately with extra cheese and black pepper on top.

Cooking Terms

Al Dente
Italian term meaning "to the tooth" - pasta cooked to be firm when bitten, not soft or mushy.
Guanciale
Italian cured meat made from pork jowl or cheeks, traditional in authentic carbonara.
Pecorino Romano
A hard, salty Italian cheese made from sheep's milk, essential for authentic carbonara flavor.
Reserve Pasta Water
Starchy water used to adjust sauce consistency and help emulsify the sauce with cheese and eggs.
